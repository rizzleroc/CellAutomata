## Title
> Test a "watch chemistry become life" science sandbox — 10-min feedback

## Reward / submissions (your call)
Suggested: **$5–10 per accepted submission, 20–40 testers.** Enough for a
statistically useful read on pricing without overspending. Mix of audiences is
fine — Q1 lets us segment afterward.

## Short description
> cellauto is a free, in-browser origin-of-life sandbox: drag two sliders and
> watch a chemistry equation self-replicate and divide into cell-like patterns.
> We're deciding what to charge for (cloud renders, HD exports, classroom tools)
> and want honest, structured feedback on the experience **and** the pricing.

## What to do (≈10 minutes)
1. Open the live demo: **https://rizzleroc.github.io/CellAutomata/**
2. Press **PLAY**. Try each **preset** (spots, stripes, mitosis, waves, labyrinth).
3. Drag the **feed (F)** and **kill (k)** sliders and watch the pattern change.
4. Scroll down to the **gallery** and the **pricing/support** section.
5. Answer the questions below.

## Feedback to submit (structured)
Answer all — short, honest answers are perfect.

**About you**
1. Which best describes you? *(hobbyist/curious · student · educator/teacher ·
   researcher · generative artist · school/museum buyer · other)*

**The experience**
2. In one word, how did the demo make you feel?
3. Did you understand what you were looking at? What confused you, if anything?
4. Any bugs, slowness, or broken UI? (device + browser, please)
5. How likely are you to share this with someone? *(0–10)* — and with whom?

**The value & pricing**
6. What single feature would make this genuinely useful to you?
7. We plan: **Pro $15/mo** (4K/poster export, cloud big-runs, save & share) and
   **Plus $5/mo** (save/share, HD export). Reactions? Too high / about right /
   too low — and why?
8. Educators only: would your school pay **~$299/yr** for a classroom license
   (lessons + teacher dashboard)? Who approves that?

**The "free taste → pay" idea (the one we most need tested)**
9. Plan: basic play is **free forever**, but cloud renders / HD exports run
   **free for ~30 seconds, then ask you to upgrade.** Does that feel:
   *totally fair · mostly fair · mildly annoying · dealbreaker* — why?
10. Which free-taste limit feels best? *15s · 30s · 60s · "first export free,
    then pay" · "just charge a flat price."*

**Commitment**
11. Would you join a waitlist / pay-what-you-want early access? *(yes/no)*

## Acceptance criteria (so you only pay for useful submissions)
- Actually opened and interacted with the demo (mentions a specific
  preset/slider/visual).
- Answers all 11 questions; Q3, Q6, Q7, Q9 have a real sentence, not one word.
- Good submissions name a concrete friction point or a concrete reason to pay.

## What we'll learn (map back to the plan)
- Q2/Q5 → does the hook land (share threshold ≥7 = 🟢).
- Q7/Q8 → is the price right (within ±30% of reactions = 🟢).
- Q9 → **does your 30s instinct hold** (≥60% "fair/mostly fair" = 🟢; mostly
  "annoying/dealbreaker" = reshape it).
- Q10 → the threshold to ship/A-B first.
- Q11 → real demand (≥25% yes = 🟢).
